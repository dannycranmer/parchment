2 Adobe Acrobat — Best for Power Users

Adobe invented the PDF format, and Acrobat remains the most feature-complete PDF editor available. It handles everything: text editing, OCR, form creation, redaction, e-signatures, accessibility tools, and more. The trade-off? It's the most expensive option here, requires a subscription, and processes files through Adobe's cloud.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free PDF editor online in 2026?

Parchment is the best free online PDF editor in 2026. It offers 15 tools — merge, split, compress, sign, watermark, protect, extract, and more — completely free, with no signup, no ads, and no file uploads. All processing happens in your browser, making it the most private option available.

Is Adobe Acrobat worth $19.99 per month?

It depends on your needs. If you edit PDF text, create complex forms, or process scanned documents with OCR every day, Adobe Acrobat Pro is worth the price. For the majority of people who just need to merge, split, or sign PDFs occasionally, it's overkill — a free tool like Parchment handles those tasks without any cost.

How does Foxit compare to Adobe Acrobat?

Foxit PDF Editor offers similar core features to Adobe Acrobat — text editing, OCR, forms, e-signatures — at a lower price ($11.99/mo vs $19.99/mo). Foxit is also lighter and faster to launch. The main advantage Adobe has is best-in-class OCR quality and a more polished mobile experience. If budget matters, Foxit is the better value.

Are online PDF tools safe for confidential documents?

Most online PDF tools (Smallpdf, Adobe online, iLovePDF) upload your files to their servers for processing. This means your documents pass through third-party infrastructure. For confidential documents — legal contracts, medical records, financial reports — use a client-side tool like Parchment or a desktop editor like Foxit that processes files locally on your device.

Which PDF editor works on Linux?

Linux users have fewer native options. Foxit offers a Linux desktop app. Parchment works on any platform via the browser. Smallpdf's web version also works on Linux. Adobe Acrobat and Nitro PDF do not have Linux versions.

Can I edit text directly in a PDF for free?

Free direct text editing in PDFs is rare. Most free tools only let you add text on top of existing content. For true text editing, you'll need a paid tool like Adobe Acrobat, Foxit PDF Editor, or Nitro PDF. If you don't need to modify existing text — just merge, split, compress, sign, or annotate — Parchment handles all of that for free.
